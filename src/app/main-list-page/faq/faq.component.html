Frequently Asked Questions Here are the answers to questions many people ask us
General questions Here are the answers to global questions about the list
Why does this list exist?
This list in a lot of aspects is ran for fun, but there is more to it than that. The aim of this list is to rank top 1 and humanly impossible levels in a ranking as accurate as we can make it. This also includes former top 1s as well.
How do you rank impossible levels?
There are a couple ways actually; harder timings (usually in the form of frame perfects), differences in length, or even if the level is 2 player or not. Factors like these and more are what make up the positions of the hardest levels on our list. The position on the list is usually determined by the staff members, who vote on a position after they playtested the level themselves. Not all rankings will be 100% accurate of course. If you want to help improve the list and correct potential misinformation, feel free to help us at our Discord server!
Why are some famous impossible levels not on the list? (Eg: TON-618, Slaughterhouse, Schafs II Gings cut)
There could be many reasons why a certain level is not on our list. A popular example would be "TON-618", this level would likely meet if it weren't for it being physically impossible and breaking clicks per second rules. "Schafs" is a similar example, breaking both cps and quality rules. Others levels get marked for removal by the staff after the level was placed on the list like in the case of Schafs II or Domba.
Why are 2 player levels higher on the list? (Eg: Codependence)
Levels with 2 player duals are typically ranked very highly on the list. The reason for this is quite simple, we judge the levels on the basis of them being played solo. For example, "Codependence" is ranked above the Demonlist arcturus because its difficulty was determined with one player controlling both icons in mind.
Why is there a 15 cps limit?
The CPS rule is in place to get rid of levels which earn their difficulty off spam entirely, think levels such as Old Slaughterhouse or Silent Circles. (SC is an example of a level which was "fixed" for this list, having a unique edited version specifically for it). The list was initially designed with more humanly feasible levels in mind, and while levels on the list are becoming harder than some of those levels. This rule is still maintained to encourage more effort in gameplay and to remain consistent with how the list has been ran since 2019.
How to have fun in the ILL?
There are many ways to enjoy Geometry Dash, some people like creating and others enjoy beating demons. Many ILL creators find that making impossible gameplay can be more interesting as you have more freedom to make the level how you want. Some other people also like to try and get world records on the levels that are on the list. There is also a group of people who like to make macros of these levels and showcase them using various tools like TASbot and Megahack v7. Due to the limitless nature of the game, there are many ways to enjoy Geometry Dash. And simply, one of those ways is found in the impossible levels community.
Creator questions Here are the answers to questions from creators/future creators
How to make a level for the ILL
To cut it short, you have to make a level that has gameplay under 15 cps, have decent decoration / gameplay and be physically possible. It also has to be harder than the current top 1 When your level is completed, join our discord server and submit in the #level-submissions channel. It'll voted on by a group of members, if it wins voting, it'll be added. The sky is your limit when it comes to gameplay creativity. You can exploit in-game bugs to create cool gameplay and not worry about it being too hard to use in a regular level.
What are the requirements for a level to be on the list?
The requirements, put simply, are: The level has to be possible on atleast 1 fps (changing fps mid-level is prohibited). The level is also required to be possible without the use of no-clip (AKA Physically possible) The level must NOT have parts that have 15cps spam or above. There are a few minor exceptions to this (swift-clicks, double-cliks) that are explained in detail in the rules below The level is also required to have presentable decoration and gameplay, that also matches the version of the level's deco (this means no wave in 1.0 styled deco levels)
Why are you wasting time making levels that nobody is going to play/not going to be rated?
This is a complex question, whose answer varies from person to person. There are many reasons why people decide to make ILL levels. Some people enjoy the concept of pushing the limits of GD and experiment with brand-new gameplay ideas. Others may want to make something different than what they normally make, and try something new by making a level not bound by difficulty they are used to building with. Other people just make impossible levels for fun. There are many more reasons on why people decide to join the ILL and make levels. If you are interested in the idea you can try it out and build an ILL level!
